# Green-Sathi
- A Project made during Alvas's Xypheria Hackathon

# Problem Statement
- Women in rural India perform 70%+ of eco-practices - composting, organic farming, waste segregation, and water conservation. They are the backbone of grassroots environmental stewardship.
- Despite their critical role, women's contributions go unrecognized in climate finance. No mechanism exists to quantify their impact or connect them to carbon credit markets.
- Zero structured reward systems exist for community-level carbon reduction. Rural women remain entirely excluded from the $114B global carbon credit market.

# The Three Critical Gaps
- No Quantification: No proper mechanism to measure women's carbon reduction impact.
- No Reward System: No structured incentives for sustainable practices at grassroots level.
- Low Awareness: Minimal understanding of carbon credits and sustainability economics.

# Mission
- Create a measurable and transparent model that tracks, verifies, and rewards eco-friendly activities performed by women - converting everyday actions into verified carbon reduction data.

# Core Value Proposition
- Simple Digital Interface: Mobile/web app for easy eco-action logging.
- SHG Network: Leverages existing Self-Help Group infrastructure for trust.
- Verified Impact: Photo + GPS + community verification for credibility.
- Direct Rewards: Carbon credits convert to cash/voucher incentives.

# How It Works
- Eco Action: Women perform sustainable practices: composting, organic farming, waste segregation, tree planting.
- Log & Capture: Record via app with photo proof, GPS location, date stamp, and action type.
- Verify: SHG coordinators review and approve submissions with community validation.
- Calculate: System converts actions to CO₂e reduction using standardized carbon accounting.
- Reward: Verified credits convert to monetary incentives.

# Features of the App
- It can be accessed in English primarily with support for Hindi and Kannada as of now.
- You can either login or register as two types of accounts: user and verifier.
- The user can log an eco-friendly action they performed using an image taken using a gps camera for verification, check their wallet for number of credits earned, check community updates, or look at training resources for increasing awareness.
- The verifier can view the actions logged by the users and either approve or reject it. The user gets rewards based on approval and how much weight their particular action contributed to the reduction of carbon credit.

# Pilot Project Impact and Outcomes
- 45 tons of carbon emission reduced.
- 5 self help groups trained on sustainability and verification.
